Strong Design, Invisible to Search Engines

Kemper Design Services has a visually impressive, conversion-focused single-page website, but it is fundamentally invisible to search engines. The site has no meta description, no schema markup, no Open Graph tags, no sitemap, no robots.txt, no canonical tag, and almost no indexable text content — because the vast majority of copy is embedded in JavaScript modals that crawlers cannot read. The design communicates high quality; the SEO infrastructure communicates nothing at all. Fixing these issues is a high-leverage opportunity: the content, services, and value props are excellent — they just need to be surfaced for search.

Key Findings
The site has a well-crafted title tag ("Kemper Design Services — AI Tools for Small Businesses") and a single, properly structured H1 ("AI That Works With Your Team"), but every other SEO signal is either missing or severely underdeveloped. The meta description is absent entirely, meaning Google will generate its own snippet — usually a poor one. There is no schema markup of any kind: no LocalBusiness, no Organization, no Service, no FAQ, and no WebSite schema, despite the site having a physical address (St Augustine, FL), a phone number, and a detailed FAQ section in a modal — all of which are prime candidates for structured data. The site has no Open Graph or Twitter Card tags, so any social sharing will produce unbranded, low-quality previews. Critically, approximately 80–85% of the site's content — including all service descriptions, the full FAQ, the About text, and the Terms & Privacy copy — lives exclusively inside JavaScript-rendered modals, which most search engine crawlers cannot read. This means the crawlable word count is roughly 420 words, far below the 1,000+ words that competitive AI services pages typically feature. The site also lacks a sitemap.xml, robots.txt, canonical tag, favicon, and any preload or compression hints. The favicon is missing entirely. On the positive side: the viewport meta tag is present, the HTML language attribute is set correctly, font preconnects are in place, social profile links (Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn, GitHub) are included in the footer, and the page structure (one H1, logical H2 use) is sound.
